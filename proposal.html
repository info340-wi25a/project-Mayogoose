Music App Proposal

Problem Description

Singers, whether performing solo or in groups, require regular vocal warm-ups to maintain vocal health, extend their range, and ensure a quality performance. However, one information problem they often face is limited access to engaging, high-quality, and diverse vocal warm-up exercises. The lack of variety can result in boredom or insufficient preparation, especially for singers in groups who may have varying ranges and skill levels. Many singers feel their warm-ups fail to adequately address their unique needs, leaving them unprepared or fatigued during performances.

Currently, singers often rely on prerecorded warm-ups available on platforms like YouTube, Spotify, and TikTok. However, with most videos lasting over 10 minutes, it is challenging to identify which exercises are included without listening to the entire video. These platforms also lack filters that support singers' specific needs. Singers often rely on keywords and algorithms to discover warm-ups that meet their specific needs, which can be time-consuming. Moreover, built-in methods in these platforms for collecting and organizing vocal warm-ups are not singer-friendly. Alternatively, singers join groups and rely on an experienced group member to lead the exercises. However, this approach often unintentionally caters to a limited vocal range or skill level, leaving some members without an effective warm-up tailored to their needs. Hence, existing solutions fall short in providing personalized, adaptive, and fun warm-up experiences for singers.

Proposed Application

We propose developing an online tool that make vocal warmup & exercises more accessible to singers.

01. Users of the application:

  1. Individual vocal hobbyists who struggles to find structured, professional vocal warm-ups on their own
  2. A group of singers (choir, a cappella) who struggles to have the perfect vocal warm-up routine that suits everyone’s need and wants

02. Interactive Features:

  1. (1 feature) Upload favorite vocal warm-ups / techniques to the crowdsourced database, adding tags, for all singers to discover and engage with.
  2. (1 feature) Record vocal warm-ups (≤ 3 minute) on the platform.
  3. (0.5 feature) Filter and organize a “vocal warm-up playlist” for choir rehearsals or personal use.
  4. (backup/optional) Individual singers in a larger group can propose or submit warm-ups for choir rehearsal, subject to approval by the admin.

03. Data / Information:

Text/prompts displayed on screen + interactive timer

  1. breath control
    • "sssssss—"
    • box breathing
    • Dog panting
  2. tongue twisters
    • “Unique New York. You need New York. You know you need unique New York.”
    • "Red leather, yellow leather. Red leather, yellow leather."
    • "She makes a proper cup of coffee in a copper coffee pot"

Audio (.mp3) or YouTube Videos)

  1. Existing vocal warm-up on YouTube
    These are YouTube video links crowdsourced by individual singers, with user defined tags, durations, and voice parts. During rehearsals or personal training, our web application will embed the YouTube video in a singer friendly format.
    • Yawn-sign
    • Straw Exercises
    • Lip Buzz
    • Tongue Trill
    • Jaw Loosening Exercise
    • Two-Octave pitch glide warm-ups
    • Vocal Sirens
    • Riff and Runs
  2. Original vocal warm-ups recorded or uploaded by users, with user defined tags, durations, and voice parts. These audio clips are under 3 minutes long and will undergo content moderation to ensure they comply with community guidelines.

04. Our Solution:

Our web-app empowers singers across level with access to engaging, high-quality, and diverse warm-up experiences.

Lowering the Barrier for Contribution

Our web-app streamline the process of producing and sharing vocal warm-ups for Content Producers, enables the singer community to crowdsource up-to-date vocal training resources more easily.

Increased Discoverability

Our web-app provide individual singers access to a diverse range of high-quality, authentic, and novel vocal warm-ups crowdsourced by experts. Singers can filter and discover new warm-ups everyday to prevent fatigue and boredom.

Usability for All

Our web-app makes it easier to engage with vocal warm-up contents by allowing singers to curate and organize their saved warm-ups into a personalized playlist. In a group setting, the "minority" voice parts can request their favorite warm-ups during the rehearsal, making sure their specific needs are addressed.
