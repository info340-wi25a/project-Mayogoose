Music App Proposal

Problem Description

Singers, whether performing solo or in groups, require regular vocal warm-ups to maintain vocal health, extend their range, and ensure a quality performance. However, they often face limited access to engaging, high-quality, and diverse vocal warm-up exercises. The lack of variety can result in boredom or insufficient preparation, especially for singers in groups who may have varying ranges and skill levels. Many singers feel their warm-ups fail to adequately address their unique needs, leaving them unprepared or fatigued during performances. Currently, singers often rely on prerecorded warm-ups available on platforms like YouTube. However, searching for and organizing warm-ups that meet their specific needs can be time-consuming. With most videos lasting over 10 minutes, it is challenging to identify which exercises are included without listening to the entire video. Alternatively, singers rely on an experienced group member to lead the exercises. However, this approach often unintentionally caters to a limited range or skill level, leaving some members without an effective warm-up tailored to their needs. Existing solutions fall short in providing personalized, adaptive, and fun warm-up experiences for singers.

App Description

We propose developing an online tool that make vocal warmup & exercises more accessible to singers.

  1. Users of the application:
    1. Individual vocal hobbyists who struggles to find structured, professional vocal warm-ups on their own
    2. A group of singers (choir, a cappella) who struggles to have the perfect vocal warm-up routine that suits everyone’s need and wants
  2. What will users do with the web app:
    1. Upload favorite vocal warm-ups / techniques to the crowdsourced database for all users to discover and engage with.n
    2. Create a “vocal warm-up playlist” for choir rehearsals or personal use.
    3. For larger groups, have an admin to approve vocal warm-ups suggested by individual singers.
  3. Data types
    1. Text/prompts displayed on screen + timer
      1. breath control
        1. "sssssss—"
        2. box breathing
        3. Dog panting
      2. tongue twisters
        1. “Unique New York. You need New York. You know you need unique New York.”
        2. "Red leather, yellow leather. Red leather, yellow leather."
        3. "She makes a proper cup of coffee in a copper coffee pot"
      3. Techniques (text + YouTube link)
        1. Yawn-sigh
        2. Humming warm-up
        3. Vocal straw exercises
        4. Lip Buzz
        5. Tongue Trill
        6. Jaw Loosening exercise
        7. Two-Octave pitch glide warm-ups
        8. Vocal Sirens
    2. audio (mp3 or YouTube links)
      1. riff and runs
      2. original vocal warm-ups recorded by users
        1. A record, edit, save, upload feature
  4. How will this web-app solve the problem
    1. Users can discover fresh vocal warm-ups everyday
    2. A group of singers can have a shared vocal warm-up playlist, like Spotify, to use during rehearsals.
